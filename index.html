Policy & Disclaimer

Educational tool — not medical advice and not a medical device. It runs on a curated demonstration dataset, can’t see your full health picture, and doesn’t cover every medicine or interaction. “No interaction found” never means a combination is safe. Never start, stop, or change a medication based on this tool — confirm everything with a pharmacist or prescriber.

What this is

A working prototype that shows how medicines may interact — grouped by the part of the body they act on (heart rhythm, brain & breathing, bleeding, kidneys, liver), with timing guidance and demographic tailoring. It reasons from well-established, mechanism-level interactions in a curated demonstration set.

What it is not

Not a validated clinical database, not a cleared medical device, not exhaustive, and not a substitute for a pharmacist or prescriber who can see your full history.

Medical disclaimer

The information here is for general education and to help you have a better-informed conversation with a qualified healthcare professional. It is not a diagnosis or a treatment recommendation. Absence of a finding is not a guarantee of safety. Always read the labeling that comes with your medicines and follow the directions of your own healthcare team.

Data & privacy

Your medication list is processed in your browser and is not sent to a server by the tool itself. Two optional features are exceptions, only when you use them: the calendar feature sends the medicines and times you choose to your own Google Calendar, and the AI explainer sends your question plus the findings already shown to an AI service to reply. A saved Medication Passport is stored locally on your device.

If you feel unwell

This tool cannot help in an emergency. If you think you are having a serious reaction — trouble breathing, chest pain, fainting, heavy bleeding, or swelling of the face or throat — call your local emergency number or go to the nearest emergency department right away.
